What Level 1 Is

Level 1 defines your personal defaults for how the Reasoning Workbench behaves when no more specific project or session settings override them. Think of it as your starting posture.

  • Scope: global for this user.
  • Effect: behaviour and presentation defaults, not authority.
  • Override model: Level 4 (session) may override for a specific workspace; Level 3 governance always binds.

Language Settings

  • Default language: your baseline language for responses (e.g. English).
  • Default language variant: spelling and dialect conventions (e.g. British English).
  • Language switching permitted: if ON, you may request a different language at any time.
  • Persist language for session: if ON, a language change persists until the session ends or is changed again.

Note: language affects output text only. It does not alter governance, reasoning rules, or authority checks.

Cognitive Profile (Cognitive Avatar)

The Cognitive Avatar defines the thinking style applied during reasoning: how work is structured, how rigor is applied, and how ambiguity is tolerated.

  • Analyst: structured, stage-disciplined, logic-first, fidelity-first. Required for Canonical Knowledge Objects (CKOs).
  • Artist: fluid, generative, coherence-first. Suitable for creative exploration only.
  • Advocate: structured but persuasion-oriented. Suitable for teaching and communication contexts.
  • Explorer: open-ended, hypothesis-preserving, tension-tolerant. Suitable for early research and sense-making.

Interaction Avatar

Interaction Avatars affect how responses are presented and how the conversation behaves (verbosity, pushback, tone). They do not alter reasoning quality or governance.

  • Minimal: answers only unless reasoning is requested.
  • Concise: brief, structured responses with light signalling.
  • Socratic: guided questioning to surface insight.
  • Didactic: full explanations shown by default.
  • Conversational: informal, rapport-oriented style.

Presentation Avatar

Presentation Avatars control layout density, scrolling behaviour, and explanation depth based on screen size and attention context.

  • Phone: ultra-compact, one idea at a time.
  • Laptop: single-screen analytical default.
  • Tablet: chunked sections for reading.
  • Multi-Screen: expanded layouts and explanations.

Epistemic, Performance, and Checkpointing Avatars

These avatars govern how truth claims are handled, how tightly work is bounded, and how checkpointing is proposed. Together they determine stability, auditability, and handoff behaviour.

Canonical work requires explicit epistemic discipline and manual checkpoint confirmation.
